Pireal was born in 2015 out of frustration. Back then, the standard tool for relational algebra practice at university was WinRDBI, Java-based, Windows-only. As a Linux user, that wasn't an option. With a background in Python and Qt, the natural response was to build something. The hardest part wasn't the interface, it was understanding how interpreters work: scanners, lexers, parsers, ASTs. That challenge became the biggest reward, building Pireal taught more about how computers actually work than anything else.
